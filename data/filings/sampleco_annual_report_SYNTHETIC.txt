SAMPLECO INDUSTRIES LTD.
ANNUAL REPORT — FISCAL YEAR 2025

LETTER FROM THE CHIEF EXECUTIVE

Fiscal 2025 was a year of disciplined execution for SampleCo. Group revenue reached $4.82 billion, up 9.4% from $4.41 billion in fiscal 2024, while operating margin expanded from 14.1% to 15.3%. We ended the year with net cash of $612 million and returned $340 million to shareholders through dividends and buybacks.

BUSINESS OVERVIEW AND REVENUE SEGMENTS

SampleCo operates three reporting segments.

Industrial Automation, our largest segment, contributed $2.31 billion in revenue (48% of group), growing 7.2% year over year. Demand was driven by factory retrofit programs in Southeast Asia and by our new ProLine-9 controller family, which shipped 118,000 units in its first full year.

Precision Components contributed $1.54 billion (32% of group), growing 6.1%. Margins in this segment recovered to 12.8% after the fiscal 2024 supply disruptions, helped by dual-sourcing of specialty alloys and the consolidation of two fabrication plants in Penang.

Digital Services, our fastest-growing segment, contributed $970 million (20% of group), up 21.5% year over year. Recurring subscription revenue now represents 64% of this segment. The SampleCloud monitoring platform passed 9,400 enterprise customers, and net revenue retention was 113%.

No single customer accounted for more than 4% of group revenue in fiscal 2025. Revenue by geography was 41% Asia-Pacific, 33% North America, 22% Europe, and 4% rest of world.
RISK FACTORS (EXTRACT)

ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE — OPPORTUNITY AND RISK

The board considers artificial intelligence both a strategic opportunity and a material risk, and reviews it twice a year.

On the opportunity side, AI-assisted predictive maintenance is now embedded in SampleCloud and contributed an estimated $86 million of fiscal 2025 Digital Services revenue. We expect this to grow as customers connect more production lines.

We identify four principal AI-related risks. First, model reliability: predictive-maintenance models that miss a failure, or alarm too often, could damage customer trust and expose us to contractual penalties; we mitigate this with human-review thresholds and staged rollouts. Second, competitive displacement: foundation-model-based tooling could commoditize parts of our Digital Services offering faster than we can differentiate; our response is to concentrate on proprietary sensor data, which generic models cannot access. Third, regulatory uncertainty: the EU AI Act and similar regimes may classify certain SampleCloud features as high-risk systems, increasing compliance cost; we have budgeted $18 million over two years for conformity work. Fourth, talent concentration: our AI engineering organization remains small relative to demand, with 214 specialists at year end, and attrition in this group runs above the company average.

The board has adopted a policy that no AI system may autonomously execute actions affecting customer production lines without a configurable human-approval step. We believe this positions SampleCo conservatively relative to peers.

CYBERSECURITY

SampleCo experienced no material cybersecurity incidents in fiscal 2025. Phishing-resistant authentication now covers 97% of employees, and our bug-bounty program paid out $1.1 million across 240 valid reports.
DIVIDEND POLICY AND CAPITAL ALLOCATION

The board's stated policy is to distribute between 40% and 50% of underlying net profit as ordinary dividends, paid semi-annually.

For fiscal 2025 the board declared a total ordinary dividend of $1.16 per share — an interim dividend of $0.52 paid in March 2025 and a final dividend of $0.64 payable in September 2025 — representing a payout ratio of 44% of underlying net profit, within the stated range. This is the eleventh consecutive year of dividend growth; the total dividend grew 7.4% over fiscal 2024's $1.08 per share.

The board may supplement ordinary dividends with share buybacks when net cash exceeds $500 million and no acquisition above $250 million is under consideration. Under this framework we repurchased $118 million of shares in fiscal 2025 at an average price of $38.20.

Dividends are declared in US dollars. Shareholders on the Singapore register may elect payment in Singapore dollars at the spot rate five business days before payment. The scrip dividend scheme remains suspended.

OUTLOOK

For fiscal 2026 we expect group revenue growth of 6% to 9%, led by Digital Services, and we expect to hold the ordinary payout ratio within the 40–50% policy range. This outlook is a planning assumption of a fictional company in a synthetic teaching document, not guidance.
